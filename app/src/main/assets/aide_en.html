Definitions
Rising / falling moon
The moon is in an ascending period when it is higher and higher in the sky from one day to the next and is in a waning period when it is not, but there is no simple way of determining at any given moment whether the moon is waxing or waning. A complete cycle lasts 27 days, 7 hours and 43 minutes. It is this aspect that is mainly used in biodynamic agriculture. The work to be done in the waning moon is as follows:
  • Transplant and plant,
  • Work the soil, bury fertilizers and soil improvers,
  • Prune to slow growth and make more vigorous,
  • Mow in the waning moon to slow the growth of the lawn,
  • Lighten,
  • Harvest the root vegetables,
  • Take the cuttings and cut,
  • Dividing and layering,
  • Collect grafts,
  • Harvest the root vegetables and bulbs.
The work to be done in the rising moon:
  • Sow,
  • Graft,
  • Harvest vegetables, flowers, fruits and leaves.
Waning / waxing moon
The moon is waxing when the visible part of it increases in size from one day to the next. This corresponds to the period between the new moon and the full moon. The ancients called this the young moon. Conversely, the moon is waning (old moon) between the full moon and the new moon, each period lasting about 14.5 days. The influence of the waxing/waning moon is not taken into account in this application. There are tricks to determine whether the moon is waxing or waning. Simply remember that the boundary between the visible part (white) and the non-visible (black) of the moon always moves from one day to the next from right (West) to left (East).
Zodiac constellations
In just over 27 days, the moon passes successively in front of the 12 constellations of the zodiac. It first rises from the middle of Sagittarius, passing through Capricorn, Aquarius, Pisces, Aries, Taurus, until the middle of Gemini. It then descends from the end of Gemini until Cancer, Leo, Virgo, Libra and Scorpio and then the beginning of Sagittarius. There is in fact a 13th constellation between Scorpio and Sagittarius, Serpentarius. Information may vary from one book to another. In Lunoid, we will consider this to be a period of a leaf-type period. It should also be noted that the boundary between two signs is not clear-cut and that a period of uncertainty of 2 or 3 hours exists at the moment of transition. We will therefore have to wait until the Moon is clearly established in a given sign.
Apogee
The moon is at its apogee when it is at its furthest from the earth, at a distance of about 406 700 km. In some documents, apogee is not considered to be a harmful period and is assimilated to a flower day.
Perigee
The moon is at perigee when it is closest to the Earth, at a distance of around 356 500 km. Plants sown during this period will tend to germinate poorly and have growth problems.
Node
A node corresponds to the moment when the moon intersects the plane of rotation of the Earth around the Sun. The node is either descending when the Moon is in descending or ascending when the Moon is rising.
Bad days
The apogee, perigee, the ascending or descending lunar nodes correspond to periods harmful to cultures and where it is necessary to abstain from all work, at least in the period between 5 hours before and 5 hours after the event.
Flower days
Period to be favored for work on flowering vegetables: cauliflower, artichoke, broccoli. The flower days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the Air element, namely Gemini, Libra or Aquarius.
Fruit days
Period to be favored for work on fruit vegetables and grains: tomato, cucumber, zucchini, beans. The fruit days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the Fire element, namely Aries, Leo or Sagittarius.
Root days
Period to be favored for work on root vegetables: radishes, potatoes, carrots. The root days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the element Earth, namely Taurus, Virgo or Capricorn.
Leaf days
Period to be favored for work on leafy vegetables: salad, sorrel, parsley. The leaf days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the Water element, namely Cancer, Scorpio or Pisces.
The Ice Saints
The Ice Saints (Saint Mamert, Saint Pancrace and Saint Servais) correspond to the period from 11, 12 and 13 May until which there is still a risk of frost. This was clearly noticeable in 2020, so it is generally advisable to wait until the second half of the second half of May to transplant tomatoes and courgettes.
Gibbous moon
The moon is gibbous when the visible part is larger than the non-visible part. In the case of a waxing moon, this corresponds to the period between the first quarter and the full moon, and in the case of a waning moon, to the period between the full moon and the last quarter.
Red moon
The red moon is the first lunation (between two new moons) after Easter. For 2024, this is the period between 8 April and 8 May. This is a period when the risk of frost for unprotected young shoots on cloudless nights. For those wondering what a religious festival has to do with it, remember that the date of Easter is calculated (according to ecclesiastical comput) as being the first Sunday after the first full moon after the first day of spring.
Blue moon
The second full moon in the same month is called a blue moon. There has been a blue moon on 31 October 2020 and the next will take place in January 2039.
Biodynamic agriculture
Introduced in 1924 by Rudolph Steiner (1861-1925) and subsequently developed from 1960 by Maria Thun (1922-2012), biodynamic agriculture is based on taking into account the influences of the Moon and the planets and the use of special preparations:
  • Preparation 500: Horn manure, for the soil and plant roots
  • Preparation 501 : Horn silica, works on the aerial parts of plants during their growth phase.
